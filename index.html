FAQs

  • How much does it cost to go solar?

    • You can go solar with zero down payment, this allows homeowners to go solar without any upfront costs.

      We'll install the panels on your roof and handle all of the costs associated with the installation and maintenance of the system. In exchange, you pay off the panels at a fixed rate, which is significantly lower than the cost of electricity from the grid. This means that from the very first month, you can start saving money on your electricity bills, and as the cost of electricity continues to rise, your savings will only increase.

  • What are the financial benefits of going solar?

    • Going solar is like having a magic sun-powered money saver on your roof. First, you can have a fixed monthly payment for your solar panels, which means you'll know exactly how much you need to pay each month - no surprises! Plus, the government gives you special rewards such as tax credits and incentives. Over time, you'll notice that your energy bills get smaller, which means more money for other things you enjoy.

      Also, having these shiny solar panels can make your house worth more, like adding an extra room or a swimming pool! So, going solar can bring lots of sunny benefits for your wallet.

  • How long do solar panels last and what is maintenance like?

    • Solar panels are designed for the long haul - they can last up to 25 years or even more. This means once installed, they'll keep soaking up sunlight and transforming it into electricity for a quarter of a century. Maintenance is typically straightforward; just keep them clean and clear of debris.

      To give you even more peace of mind, we offer a 10-year workmanship warranty. This ensures that if any issues arise from the installation process itself, we'll be there to fix them at no extra cost to you.

  • What is net metering?

    • Net metering is a billing arrangement with your electric utility. When your solar panels generate more electricity than your home is using, the excess power is fed back into the electric grid. You receive a credit from your utility for this excess power.

      Later, when your panels aren't producing enough electricity (such as at night or during cloudy weather), you can use the credits to draw power from the grid at no extra cost.

      Net metering can help to offset the cost of your solar system, making solar a more attractive investment.
